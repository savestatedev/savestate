SaveState v0.8.0: Break Free ‚Äî Full Migration Between ChatGPT and Claude

You've spent months training your AI. Your custom instructions are dialed in. Your memories are rich. Now you want to try a different platform. Today, that doesn't mean starting over.

The Problem

Here's the reality: you're locked in.

Every conversation you've had, every preference your AI has learned, every custom instruction you've painstakingly refined ‚Äî it's all trapped inside one platform. Want to try Claude's new capabilities? Switch to ChatGPT for a specific use case? You're looking at starting from scratch.

Your AI knows how you like code formatted, what frameworks you prefer, your communication style, your project context. That's not something you can recreate in an afternoon. It takes weeks. Sometimes months.

Platform lock-in isn't a feature ‚Äî it's a trap. And today, we're giving you the key.

The Solution

SaveState v0.8.0 introduces full bidirectional migration between ChatGPT and Claude. Two commands. That's it.

# Moving to Claude
savestate migrate --from chatgpt --to claude

# Going back to ChatGPT
savestate migrate --from claude --to chatgpt

Your custom instructions, memories, project knowledge, and files ‚Äî they all come with you. No manual export. No copy-pasting. No losing context.

This isn't a one-way door. Try Claude for a week. Go back to ChatGPT. Migrate again. Your AI state is portable. As it always should have been.

What Actually Migrates

We didn't cut corners. Here's what transfers:

  • Custom Instructions / System Prompts ‚Äî Your carefully crafted personality and behavioral guidelines translate between platforms
  • Memories / Project Knowledge ‚Äî Everything your AI has learned about you, your preferences, and your projects
  • Files and Attachments ‚Äî Documents, code files, and reference materials you've uploaded

Before migration completes, you get a compatibility report showing exactly what transfers perfectly and what needs attention. No surprises.

Dry Run Mode

Not ready to commit? Run --dry-run to preview exactly what will change ‚Äî without touching anything.

savestate migrate --from chatgpt --to claude --dry-run

Resume Interrupted Migrations

Laptop died mid-migration? Network hiccup? Run the command again and pick up right where you left off. No duplicate data, no corrupted state.

Intelligent Content Adaptation

Platforms have different limits and formats. Claude handles system prompts differently than ChatGPT. Memory entry sizes vary. The migration wizard handles all of this automatically ‚Äî reformatting, chunking, and adapting content to fit the target platform's constraints.

Your Data Stays Yours

Let's be crystal clear: your data never touches our servers.

The migration wizard runs entirely on your machine. Client-side encryption means your custom instructions, memories, and files are never transmitted to SaveState infrastructure. We don't see your data. We can't see your data. That's by design.

This is your AI. Your context. Your choice where it lives.

Get Started

Upgrade to v0.8.0:

# npm
npm install -g @savestate/cli@latest

# Homebrew
brew upgrade savestate

# Verify
savestate --version

Then run your first migration:

# Preview what will migrate
savestate migrate --from chatgpt --to claude --dry-run

# Run the migration
savestate migrate --from chatgpt --to claude

That's it. Your AI context, now portable.
